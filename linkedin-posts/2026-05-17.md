You won't see it in a velocity chart.
You won't see it in a sprint report.
You'll see it in things that should've been obvious — and weren't.

Here's what context loss looks like at scale:

**01 — The same PR, written twice**
Different agents, different branches, same problem — solved twice. You merge one. The other shows up next week, looking familiar.

**02 — Two agents, two conflicting patterns**
One follows your repo pattern. The other rolls its own. Both pass review. Now your codebase has two truths — the next agent picks at random.

**03 — Tribal knowledge nobody can point to**
Why retries work that way. Why an endpoint has a 2-second SLA. It lives in a senior engineer's head — and the agent can't ask.

**04 — Onboarding that resets every sprint**
Every new agent rediscovers your architecture from scratch. Knowledge lives nowhere durable — and gets re-paid every time.

**05 — Incidents with no audit trail**
Production breaks. You trace it to an AI-generated PR. Which agent? Which policy? What context? Silence.
